ON BEING THE RIGHT SIZE

J. B. S. Haldane

THE most obvious differences between different animals are differences of size, but for some reason the zoologists have paid singularly little attention to them[J. L.1] . In a large textbook of zoology before me I find no indication that the eagle is larger than the sparrow, or the hippopotamus bigger than the hare, though some grudging admissions are made in the case of the mouse and the whale[J. L.2] . But yet it is easy to show that a hare could not be as large as a hippopotamus, or a whale as small as a herring[J. L.3] . For every type of animal there is a most convenient size, and a large change in size inevitably carries with it a change of form[J. L.4] .


Let us take the most obvious of possible cases, and consider a giant man sixty feet high-about the height of Giant Pope and Giant Pagan in the illustrated Pilgrim's Progress of my childhood[J. L.5] . These monsters were not only ten times as high as Christian; but ten times as wide and ten times as thick, so that their total weight was a thousand times his, or about eighty to ninety tons[J. L.6] . Unfortunately[J. L.7]  the cross-sections of their bones[J. L.8]  were only a hundred times those of Christian, so that[J. L.9]  every square inch of giant bone had to support ten times the weight borne by a square inch of human bone. As the human thigh-bone breaks under about ten times the human weight[J. L.10] , Pope and Pagan would have broken their thighs every time they took a step[J. L.11] . [J. L.12] This was doubtless why they were sitting down in the picture I remember. But it lessens one's respect for Christian and Jack the Giant Killer.[J. L.13]

 [J. L.2]Sentence 2: (a). Stands up the general accusation in sentence 1; (b). Qualifies the challenge – with quite a sting in the tail.

 [J. L.4]Sentence 4: (a). Makes two claims that will have to be justified; (b). Back to the general.

 [J. L.5]Sentence 5: (a). Immediately connects to the reader….; (b). …and personalises “my childhood”; (c) Back to the specific again.

 [J. L.6]Sentence 6: (a). Explains how monster dimensions work… (b). …and gives us the outcome, without resorting to equations.

L.7]“Unfortunately” – one of those wonderful little contrast words that send a signal to the reader: “oh, oh, something’s going to happen here.” Other words in this category are “And” and “But” at the start of a sentence, “however” in the middle. “Moreover” – strengthens what you are saying; “In contrast” – weakens it (but in a strong way).
